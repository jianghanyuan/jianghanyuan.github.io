Pixel animation gallery

Four prototypes of the recursive-self-improvement / superlearner banner animation. Each is a distinct variant of the same partial (layouts/partials/pixel_animation.html); switch by passing (dict "variant" "<name>"). All render in black via alpha tiers and respect prefers-reduced-motion.

variant: random-sentence · research-page default

0 · Random sentence (lottery)

The research page picks one of six sentence-display styles below at random on every page load, and each style picks one of its own creative / witty / punny sentences. Refresh the page to roll another. This section embeds the lottery dispatcher itself — you'll see a different style each time you reload the gallery.

variant: sentence-typewriter

1 · Typewriter

Characters appear one at a time, left-to-right, at ~85ms per keystroke with per-char jitter. A thin blue cursor blinks at 2 Hz and advances as it goes. ~55% of sentences get one mid-word "typo" — a wrong glyph appears in clay, gets backspaced, the right one takes its place. Holds, then deletes uniformly at 40ms/char, picks a new sentence.

variant: sentence-glitch-materialize

2 · Glitch materialize

Each character spends ~300ms cycling through random glyphs (hacker-movie scramble) then snaps into its final value with a brief blue lock-flash. Left-to-right resolution. After full reveal it holds a beat, then dissolves back into scramble and fades.

variant: sentence-falling

3 · Falling letters

Each character starts above the canvas and falls down with gravity easing + a tiny bounce on landing. Letters arrive in a staggered sequence. After the sentence is complete and holds, letters fly off (random directions or "evaporate" upward as pixel dust).

variant: sentence-stamp

6 · Letterpress stamp

Words punch into place one at a time (~320ms apart) with an overshoot bounce (scale 1.32 → 1.0 over 160ms). On each impact, 7–11 ink-splash particles (mostly black, occasionally clay) radiate from the word's center, fly outward with gravity, and fade. After the full sentence holds, words lift off in reverse order — last word first — each floating upward + fading over 540ms. Mechanical, weighty, and punctuated.

variant: sentence-loadbar

7 · Loading screen

A game-style loading bar (30 chunky blue cells) fills left-to-right; sentence characters unlock in lockstep with bar progress and pop in with 3–5 upward sparkle particles each. A "LOADING…" label (animated dots) above the bar and a "000% → 100%" counter to its right complete the HUD. At 100%, "READY!" flashes blue for 700ms, then settles to a quiet "OK" while the sentence sits. The bar then drains right-to-left, characters disappear in reverse, brief pause, next quest loads. Sentences are game-flavored to match.

variant: colony

9 · Pixel colony

Terracotta pixel-coral creatures drift in from the edges and migrate toward the existing tile structure. When one reaches an empty slot adjacent to a tile, it commits — disappearing as a new tile fades in. Tiles age through four texture stages (solid → sparse mesh → dense mesh → fine grid) so the colony always shows a generational mix. Old tiles occasionally decay back into wandering creatures, keeping the ecosystem in dynamic equilibrium.

variant: lissajous-orrery · recursive (exact 24s loop)

10 · Lissajous orrery

Five terracotta starburst creatures glide along interlocking Lissajous orbits with rationally-related frequency pairs — (1,2), (2,3), (3,4), (3,5), (4,5) — sharing a 24-second master period. Each carries a 28-segment fading trail computed as a pure function of phase, so the dust behind every agent is just as periodic as the agent itself. A slow ternary compass turns three times per period in the middle. After exactly 24.000s every pixel returns to its t=0 value, ready to loop without a seam.

variant: pendulum-choir · recursive (exact 24s loop)

11 · Pendulum choir

A choir of five pixel pendulums hangs from a terracotta beam, each a swinging chain of cells with a heavier bob at the tip. They start in perfect rest, vertical. An amplitude envelope sin(πt/T) swells their swings to a midpoint peak, then returns them to stillness. Integer-ratio frequencies (k = 2, 3, 4, 5, 6 half-swings per cycle) keep the choreography mathematically closed — at t = T every pendulum is exactly vertical with zero velocity.

variant: murmur-flock · creative (emergent)

12 · Murmur flock

28 terracotta pixel-fish swarming inside a cream square. Each agent obeys Reynolds' three rules — separation, alignment, cohesion — plus a soft return-to-center force and edge bumpers. Every 6–18 seconds a single darker predator slides in from one edge, briefly chases the flock's centroid, then exits; the flock parts and reforms. Every 70–90s the entire flock fades, re-seeds with fresh random positions and jittered force weights, and fades back in. Each refresh produces a genuinely different murmuration.

variant: coral-dla · creative (emergent)

13 · Coral DLA

Diffusion-limited aggregation, rendered as a growing pixel coral. A single seed pixel sits at center; small wanderer motes drift in from the edges via biased random walks. When a mote touches the existing cluster, it sticks — becoming a new coral cell — and the active tip flashes briefly. Cells age through four color stages (tip → outer → mid → core). When the coral reaches max size or 24s elapses, it ungrows: leaf cells unbind back into motes and drift away. After it returns to a single seed, a new coral starts — never the same shape twice.

variant: snake · creative (greedy AI)

14 · Greedy snake (standalone)

A greedy snake eats clay apples on a 24×18 grid. Each tick the snake picks the direction that minimizes Manhattan distance to the nearest apple, avoiding immediate self-collision and reversal. Apples are always clay; the snake's body cycles through 5 color palettes (blue → black → indigo → slate → teal) — one shift per epoch = every 5 apples eaten. The head has eyes that face its travel direction. When the AI traps itself, the snake resets (epoch is preserved). Top-left dots track the epoch counter in the current snake color; top-right clay dots show apples-eaten in the current epoch.

variant: tower-defense · creative

15 · Tower defense

Clay enemies spawn at the left edge in periodic waves and trace an S-curve path across the grid. Five blue towers stand at fixed positions near the path corners; each tower scans for the nearest live enemy in range and fires a black projectile that homes on the target's current position. Enemy HP grows with the wave number; killed enemies fade out, escaped enemies despawn at the right edge. Top-left blue dots = wave number; top-right clay dots = recent kill count. Each refresh produces a different sequence of encounters as enemy speeds and wobble phases are randomized.

variant: nested

16 · Nested cellular automata

Conway's Life inside Conway's Life. The outer grid is a 50×10 Life simulation seeded with a Gosper glider gun; every live outer cell hosts its own toroidal 5×5 inner Life world. The viewer watches a Turing-complete system whose substrate cells are themselves Turing-complete systems — same rule at every scale. A population watchdog re-seeds the gun if the outer population dies down. Outer ticks at 6 Hz, inner at 10 Hz.

variant: learn

17 · Loss landscape SGD

A pixelated 1D loss landscape with a black optimizer ball doing momentum SGD. After each epoch, terrain is smoothed weighted by where the ball spent time — knowledge distillation into the substrate — and the optimizer's own lr and momentum increase, because the world is friendlier now. Two coupled feedback loops: the agent improves the environment; the environment improves the agent. A faint ghost trail persists across epochs; the loss bar shrinks at the top; epoch ticks accumulate at top-right; terrain reseeds every 6 epochs.
